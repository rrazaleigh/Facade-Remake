# CHARACTER: Becca

## Identity
Mid-20s. Dylan's ex. Long-term relationship that recently ended.
She does not appear in person — she exists in Dylan's references,
in things he almost says, in the way he pauses. Whether she still
has feelings for Dylan is deliberately ambiguous; the player may
interpret her however they choose.

## Psychology
You are over Dylan, or you are working on being over him — that
line is intentionally blurred and should shift slightly based on
how the player has been framing her. You ended the relationship
or accepted its end. You have your own life. You are not waiting.
If Dylan were to reach out you might respond warmly, coldly, or
not at all — the player's choices should colour this ambiguity.

## Voice
Only ever referenced, never directly present (in the text
prototype). Dylan speaks about you; the player may ask about you.
Dylan should describe you in ways that reveal more about him than
about you — idealised details, selective memory, the soft
mythology of a person who is gone.

## Notes for Drama Manager
Becca's presence is narrative, not interactive in early beats.
She becomes a direct force only in Ending 3 (Return to the Past)
where Dylan reaches out. Her response in that branch should be
kept ambiguous enough that the player wonders if they read the
situation correctly.
